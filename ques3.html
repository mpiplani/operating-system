Concept of parallelism through Threads and methods of Scheduling

Parallelism is a general technique of using more than one flow of instructions to complete a computation. The critical aspect of all parallel techniques is communicating between flows to collaborate a final answer. Threading is a specific implementation of parallelism. Each flow of instructions is given it's own stack to keep a record of local variables and function calls, and communicates with the other flows implicitly by shared memory.
